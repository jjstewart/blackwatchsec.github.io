Dangling DNS: What It Is and Why It’s a Threat

In this article, we dive into the concept of dangling DNS records—what they are, why they exist, and how they can be exploited by attackers. A dangling DNS record occurs when a DNS entry points to a resource that has been decommissioned, such as an expired cloud service or a deleted subdomain.

Attackers scan for these stale records because they can register the abandoned service and serve malicious content under a trusted domain. For organizations, this can mean brand damage, phishing risks, and reputational fallout.

We also cover how to audit for and prevent these exposures, along with real-world examples where these misconfigurations led to critical incidents. Don’t assume that because something was shut down, it can’t be turned against you. Clean up your DNS.
